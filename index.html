Why mentor?

Mentor because...

Develop skills

Nurture diversity & inclusion

We know intuitively that diversity matters, but the introduction of the varying perspectives that come with diversity is a marker in measuring tangible success, even financial success

Mentor better

Formulate good responses

Approachable

Vulnerable

Encouraging

Walk the middle path

Work on your emotional intelligence

Body language is important; eye contact, oen/vulnerable stance

Do not shame anyone in their learning process

Non-directive questions

Avoid the trap of too much specificity

Formulate good responses

  • ✗ Well, actually...
  • ✗ It's easy.
  • ✔ What solutions have you considered?
  • ✔ What do you need from me to feel supported?

Often provides minor corrections not conducive to furthering understanding

Could imply they shouldn't need help

Validates their input as capable & valuable

Jump-starts ideas/discussion

Mentor better...

Practice self care

Mentor better...

Colleague first, mentor second

Mentor better...

Focus on character

Build them up so that they can make good decisions without you

Your goal is for them to rely on you less & less

Mentor better...

Forever mentor

Allows for long-term goals

We all benefit

Healthy communities

Social capital

Collective growth

Ultimately, mentoring is about building healthy tech communities that run on social capital & thrive on collective growth. Mentoring develops a trust for candor & openness in our community that compounds with time & allows for greater connections & greater successes.
