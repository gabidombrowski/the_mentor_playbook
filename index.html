Non-directive

Body language is important; eye contact, open stance, active listening

Do not shame anyone in their learning process

Formulate good responses

Be receptive and don't be surprised

Be especially encouraging to the first few questions

Walk the middle path

What might seem obvious to you might not be obvious to them, blindspots

Avoid the trap of too much specificity

Formulate good responses

  • ✗ Well, actually...
  • ✗ It's easy.
  • ✗ Just XYZ...
  • ✔ What have you considered already?
  • ✔ What do you need to feel supported?
  • ✔ When X, do Y, because Z...

Often provides minor corrections not conducive to furthering understanding

Could imply the learner shouldn't need help

Non-directive instruction

Validate their input as intelligent and valuable, jump-starts ideas and discussion

Collective growth

Ultimately, mentoring is about building healthy tech communities that run on social capital & thrive on collective growth. Mentoring develops a trust for candor & openness in our community that compounds with time & create space for greater connections & greater successes.
