Mentor Director, Mentor Playbook

Goal of talk: to demistify mentoring & encourage others to mentor

What is mentoring?

Mentoring is supporting someone in order to facilitate their journey to getting where they've self-directed is their destination - it is not telling someone what to do or having all the answers. What's important is that you're there to provide support and let them learn from your experience.

The Learning Process

Stages of Competence

ANYTHING you've ever learned or will learn will take you through these stages and all their frustrations

Conscious incompetence: Easy to get overwhelmed; Need resources & discipline

Conscious competence: Takes time & motivation; Need practice & support

Unconscious competence: Need continued practice (can slide backwards if skill is not used)

What'll make the difference in whether you give up or move to the next level is perseverance & support

This helps you develop empathy for yourself & others through the learning process

A mentor is someone who supports you through this process & helps keep you motivated so you don't give up

If you have a handful or half a handful of things that you've reached this level at in your field, you've familiarized yourself with the learning process and can help others through it

Am I ready?

It's about two people in a developmental relationship who are supporting mutual learning and growth

How should we mentor?

We tend to treat mentoring as if it's an innate skill, or an easy skill, and it's not - it's a skill like any other that needs to be learned, practiced, and improved with time

Formulate good responses

Approachable

Vulnerable

Present

Approachable: Non-judgemental; do not shame anyone in their learning process

Vulnerable: Share your own failures & mishaps

Present: Shows that you're receptive and available

Body language is important; eye contact, open stance, active listening; 65% is non-verbal

Formulate good responses

Encouraging

Walk the middle path

Non-directive

Be especially encouraging to the first few questions

Walk the middle path: don't go too deep or too shallow in your explanations; Avoid the trap of too much specificity so you don't overwhelm them

Do not assume that you know what's best for them, what they need needs to be self-determined by them

Answers need to be non-directive to help them reach a conclusion

Formulate bad responses

  • ✗ Well, actually...
  • ✗ It's easy.
  • ✗ Just XYZ...

Well, actually: Often provides minor corrections not conducive to furthering understanding

It's easy: Could imply it's so easy, the learner shouldn't need help

Just XYZ...: Might solve the situation but is not transferrable

Formulate good responses

  • ✔ What do you need to feel supported?
  • ✔ When X, do Y, because Z...

When X, do Y, because Z...: Great for code reviews & other technical situations

Mentor better

Mentor better...

Self care

Lead by example to create a healthy relationship/community

Take breaks when needed - you can't pour from an empty cup

Know your limits, set & maintain boundaries

Don't know?

Hit Pause

'Hit Pause' when you don't have the right information, experience, or emotional state to provide a good answer to give you a chance to use your resources and come back with a clear and valuable response

Saying "I don't know" makes you relatable, it would be intimidating if you knew all the things

Mentor better...

Cultural & personal sensitivities

What is their body language communicating

Keep a lookout for your own blindspots

Acknowledge & appreciate differences, in perspectives as well

They've had different experiences and WILL have different experiences even with the same actions

Outlive the relationship

Future benefits

Forever mentor

Long-term guidance, which will help them make decisions that outlive their time with you

Ask for feedback

Reiterate and improve based on that feedback
